§ 01 — premise

Started in CSP go-to-market — selling fiber buildout to rural carriers that moved at the pace of capital expenditure cycles, not roadmaps. Learned how to talk infrastructure with engineers and ROI with CFOs in the same breath. That gap between the two is where most deals die.

Moved into tribal IT and network architecture. Built systems for sovereign nations with constrained budgets, complex regulatory requirements, and zero tolerance for consultants who couldn't ship. The work was real. The feedback was immediate.

Now running Tolowa Pacific as the base, Konative as the intel layer, SpokaneWire as the local signal experiment, and MOTION as the operating system underneath it all. The through-line: systems that run without someone manually running them.
